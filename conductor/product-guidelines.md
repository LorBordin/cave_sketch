# CaveSketch Product Guidelines

## Design Philosophy
CaveSketch should feel like a helpful assistant in the field. It prioritizes clarity and ease of use over complex feature sets, ensuring that users can go from a raw data export to a finished visualization with minimal cognitive load.

## User Experience (UX)
- **Low Friction**: Minimize the number of steps required to achieve a core task (e.g., generating a PDF). Use sensible defaults for scales and rotations.
- **Mobile-Friendly**: Ensure that all UI components are accessible and readable on mobile devices.
- **Immediate Feedback**: Provide clear status updates and rapid rendering previews so users know their actions are working.

## Visual Aesthetic
- **Clean & Modern**: Use a minimalist design that focuses on the survey data. Avoid unnecessary clutter or decorative elements.
- **Clarity over Flash**: Use high-contrast colors for survey lines and clear typography for labels and scales.
- **Standard UI**: Leverage standard Streamlit components to maintain a familiar and predictable interface.

## Prose & Tone
- **Friendly & Guiding**: Use an approachable tone that encourages users. Explain technical terms (like "georeferencing" or "affine transform") simply when necessary.
- **Action-Oriented**: Focus on what the user can *do*. Use clear, verb-led instructions (e.g., "Upload your DXF", "Download PDF").
- **Multilingual Support**: Maintain support for both English and Italian, ensuring that tone remains consistent across translations.
